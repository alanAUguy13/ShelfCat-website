The ShelfCat Roadmap

A unified plan for scaling autonomous enforcement across retail, commercial buildings, industry, and federal facilities.

Year 1 — Foundation & Validation

Months 1–12 · Establish the enforcement core and validate energy savings.

  • 3 pilot sites deployed (office, cold chain, federal)
  • Policy Engine v1.0 with 50+ energy codes
  • <50ms enforcement latency achieved in bench tests
  • Full enforcement pipeline operational at all sites
  • ≥15% energy waste reduction demonstrated
  • Interim Technical Report delivered to DOE

Year 2 — Expansion & Intelligence

Months 13–24 · Multi-sector deployment and AI refinement.

  • 15 sites across 5 sectors (office, data center, cold chain, industrial, federal)
  • Federated learning activated across all sites
  • ≥8% accuracy improvement vs. individual-site models
  • RL policy optimizer integrated
  • ≥20% false-positive reduction at RL-enabled sites
  • 2 peer-reviewed papers submitted
  • ORNL validation per ASHRAE Guideline 14

Year 3 — National Scale & Open Science

Months 25–36 · Federal integration, open-science publication, and OEM launch.

  • 30+ sites deployed nationally
  • 3 Federal FBPS buildings fully integrated
  • 200+ energy codes ingested into Policy Engine
  • ASC open-science publication (models, FAISS index, NEVPD)
  • 2 provisional patents filed
  • 4 additional peer-reviewed papers submitted
  • Siemens OEM licensing agreement executed
  • GSA Schedule filing submitted

Long-Term Vision

The future of ShelfCat extends beyond enforcement — toward universal trusted data.

  • ByteTrack + Manifest Hashing for tamper-proof audit trails
  • Robotics integration (Tesla Optimus → ShelfCat Enforcement)
  • Quantum-ready architecture for next-generation inference
  • National Energy Violation Pattern Database powering DOE research
  • Autonomous compliance across all physical infrastructure sectors
